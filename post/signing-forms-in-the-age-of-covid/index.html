    Signing Forms in the Age of COVID

    May 31, 2020

    We are living in a new world of contactless delivery, paperless billing, and curbside car sales. Why should your forms be any different?

    Paper Forms in a Contactless Age

    We recently spoke to a school principal who was distributing iPads and laptops to students to help them with distance learning. The form he had to use required the signature of the student, the parent, and a school employee, ensuring close contact with three different people. “In a time when we need to limit contact with others, three of us signing a form, potentially with the same pen, seems careless,” he said.

    IEP meetings are another time when multiple people have to sign the same document. Before the pandemic, this was an easy - passing a piece of paper around a conference table. Now, most people are likely in different places, making this a complicated mess of printing, signing, and scanning, and it is only possible if all people involved have access to a printer and scanner.

    Possible Solutions

    Most big document signers give you two types of options for digital signing, but both of these require more work than you think.

    A Single, Uploaded Form

    One option is to upload a single form, for instance, a device checkout form. When it comes time for the parent to sign, they authenticate, fill out the fields of the form, and sign it digitally.

    While this means your employees don’t have to put in a lot of work, your parents will. For the parents of three or four students in your school, these form fields can mean a lot of added time.

    Lots of Uploading

    If you want to take the burden off your parents, you can opt for the Big Box’s other solution, uploading many forms. You might use a digital IEP software to create a notice of invitation for an individual child’s meeting, upload it, and then send the parent a link to sign it.

    This method eliminates all the unnecessary fields that your parents have to fill out, but it does mean your employees are uploading a lot of files. What if everyone in the district checks out a device, or you want to incorporate your media release form into this process? Now your people are spending too much time uploading forms.

    Schoork - The Best of Both

    Neither of those solutions seems just right; they both have significant drawbacks. Schoork allows you to take the best of both solutions, reducing the work on your employees and your parents.

    Automating Form Creation

    Each form, whether it’s a device checkout form or an IEP meeting attendance form, will have some specific parameters. The employee enters these through the same familiar Schoork interface they use.

    In the case of an IEP form, which might have a student’s name, grade, school, or parent’s information, this data doesn’t need to be entered every time since it is tied to the student. The employee gets all of this extra data added to the form for free by selecting the student’s name. Now all that’s left is for the parent to sign the form.

    Keeping it Secure

    Besides having to sign into Schoork to sign a form, parents and employees have to enter a signing password whenever they sign a document or form. Schoork stores the signing password in an encrypted format, meaning the password is secure from forgery. This password ensures that parents intend to sign the document (a requirement of the E-Sign Act of 2000).
